Emmanuel Nene Odjidja

M&E specialist · researcher · epidemiologist · Geneva

I'm a monitoring and evaluation specialist. I design and run evaluations of programmes in fragile and conflict-affected settings, mostly in West Africa, the Sahel, and East Africa. I lead research, design, and learning at GCERF in Geneva, and edit the case-based evaluations section of the Journal of MultiDisciplinary Evaluation.

If a programme works, it should be proven using sound evidence, not anecdotes. That's the through-line of twelve years of work — across South Sudan, Burundi, Ghana, the Sahel, Tunisia, and Sri Lanka — testing methods where they are likeliest to fail.

I read epidemiology at Queen Margaret University in Edinburgh. I work in English and French. I write half-marathons. The full marathon remains theoretically appealing.

What I do

Most of my work is matched difference-in-differences, propensity-score matching, event-study designs, and mixed methods. I choose methods for what the setting can support, not for what looks rigorous on paper. Most of what I've learned about evaluation I learned by watching well-specified designs collapse on contact with the field, then asking what salvageable evidence remained.
